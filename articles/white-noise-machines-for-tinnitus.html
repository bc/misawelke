White Noise Machines For Tinnitus: The Premiere Sound Machine Guide

Have you considered trying a white noise machine, also referred to as a "sound machine", to treat your tinnitus? With so many different options available on the market, it may seem difficult to know which sound machine is best for treating tinnitus.

Sound machines range in stimulus, price, and features such as whether they are portable or Bluetooth compatible. It's necessary to consider your unique needs when selecting the right sound machine to treat your tinnitus.

Sound machines produce a soft, soothing sound that is referred to as the "stimulus." Sometimes the stimulus is also referred to as "masking."

What is a tinnitus masker?

The sound stimulus may be white noise, pink noise, ocean or waves, leaves rustling in the wind, or other types of relaxing sound that aims to cover, mask, or blend the tinnitus such that it is less noticeable or bothersome.

What is white noise, anyway?

The most important considerations for selecting the sound stimulus are that the sound should feel soothing and be kept at a relatively low volume.  You may still hear your tinnitus at times while using a white noise machine, and that's okay.

You should feel relaxed while you are using a noise or sound machine. While using a sound machine, try not to engage in active listening or conversation, and instead focus on feeling relaxation and relief.

Tips for Using White Noise Machines for Tinnitus Treatment

‍

In addition to using a white noise machine for tinnitus, there are a number of strategies and considerations that you can use to optimize your tinnitus management. Sound therapy for tinnitus is a powerful tool for managing tinnitus intrusion.

Consider the following tips:

Optimize White Noise Machine Use

  • Reduce other background noise while using a white noise machine
  • Set the volume to an audible but comfortable level, it's okay if you can still hear your tinnitus sometimes while using white noise therapy
  • Pick a stimulus that is pleasant but not interesting (e.g. don't listen to music that you'll sing along with)
  • If using a timer, set the timer for longer than you think you'll need to fall asleep so as to give yourself coverage if you wake up while falling asleep
  • If traveling with a portable white noise machine, pack extra batteries or make sure the device is fully charged before departing
  • You may benefit from the convenience of using multiple sound machines for different situations (e.g. one for the bedroom, a second one for the office or a portable one for travel)
